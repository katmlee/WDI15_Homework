Anita Borg

Computer Scientist & Founder of IWT

If we want technology to serve society rather than enslave it, we have to build systems accessible to all people - be they male or female, young, old, disabled, computer wizards or technophobes.

Quick Facts

  • Birth: January 17, 1949
  • Birth Place: Illinois, USA
  • Alma Mater: Ph.D New York University.
  • Random Facts: She taught herself how to program while working at an insurance company. In 1999, President Bill Clinton appointed her to the Presidential Commission on the Advancement of Women and Minorities in Science, Engineering, and Technology.
  • Contribution: Creator of Systers mailing lists. Developed MECCA, an email and web-based system for communicating with virtual communities. CTO at Xerox. Founder of Institute for Women and Technology and Grace Hopper Celebration of Women in Computing.
